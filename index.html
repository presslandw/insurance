Common Questions

Straight answers to the questions I hear most often.

Why use an independent broker instead of going directly to a company?

An independent broker represents you, not the insurance company. We compare options across Canada's top carriers to find the best coverage and rates for your specific needs. You get unbiased advice and more choice, often at a lower cost than going direct.

Bank mortgage insurance vs. independent protection: What's the difference?

Bank insurance protects the bank's loan, not your family. The benefit decreases as you pay down your mortgage, but your premiums stay the same. Independent coverage pays your family directly, stays level, and is portable if you switch lenders. It's almost always the superior choice for BC homeowners. Compare the two →

How does the First Home Savings Account (FHSA) work in BC?

The FHSA allows first-time buyers to save up to $8,000/year ($40,000 lifetime) with tax-deductible contributions and tax-free growth. It's a powerful tool that I help clients integrate into their broader financial and insurance strategy to hit down payment goals faster. Read the FHSA guide →

What if I have health issues or pre-existing conditions?

One of the biggest advantages of an independent broker is our access to specialty markets. Even if you've been declined by a bank or another insurer, we often find "simplified issue" plans that require no medical exams or specialize in specific health histories.

Term vs. Whole Life: Which one do I actually need?

Term Life is affordable and perfect for temporary needs (like a mortgage), while Whole Life provides permanent protection and a cash value component. Most young families start with Term, but the "right" choice depends entirely on your specific goals. Get a personal recommendation →
