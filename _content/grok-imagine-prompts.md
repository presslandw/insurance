# Insight Thumbnail Prompts for Grok Imagine

Use these prompts in Grok Imagine. Request square format (1:1 aspect ratio) for all.

The goal is a consistent editorial photography feel across all five: warm natural light, shallow depth of field, muted earth tones with hints of copper/gold, no text or logos in the image.

---

## 1. FHSA — "Your secret weapon for a first home down payment"
**File:** insight-fhsa.png

> Editorial photograph, young couple walking through a charming residential neighbourhood in autumn, holding hands, looking at houses with quiet optimism. Warm golden hour light filtering through maple trees. Shot from behind at a slight distance, candid and natural. Soft bokeh background. Muted earth tones, warm palette. No text. Photorealistic, 35mm film quality.

**Why:** Captures the aspiration of home ownership without the cliché of standing in front of a house holding a sign. The autumn maple trees also subtly read as Canadian.

---

## 2. Term vs. Whole Life — "Term vs. Whole Life explained"
**File:** insight-life.png

> Editorial photograph, a father sitting on a porch with his young daughter on his lap, both looking out at a backyard garden at golden hour. Warm, intimate, candid moment. Shallow depth of field, soft natural light. Earth tones, copper and green accents. No text. Photorealistic, shot on 50mm lens.

**Why:** Family protection is emotional, not technical. This anchors it in a quiet, real moment rather than a posed family portrait. The current image is decent so this is optional — only replace if you want consistency across the full set.

---

## 3. Disability — "Protecting your ability to earn"
**File:** insight-disability.png (definitely replace this one)

> Editorial photograph, a skilled tradesperson's hands resting on a woodworking bench at the end of a workday, tools nearby, warm workshop light coming through a window. Close-up detail shot. Sawdust particles catching the light. Warm amber tones, shallow depth of field. No people's faces, no text. Photorealistic, tactile and grounded.

**Why:** Disability insurance is about protecting your ability to work. Showing capable hands at rest — rather than someone in a rehab clinic — communicates the value of what's being protected, not the fear of losing it. Much more dignified.

## 4. Mortgage — "Bank vs. Independent: Which coverage is safe?"
**File:** insight-mortgage.png

> Editorial photograph, a warm and inviting front door of a craftsman-style home, slightly ajar, with soft interior light spilling out onto a welcome mat. Evening twilight, porch light glowing. Potted plants beside the door. Shallow depth of field. Warm earth tones, copper and amber hues. No people, no text. Photorealistic, 35mm lens.

**Why:** The current image is too similar to the FHSA thumbnail (family in front of a house). This one evokes home and security through atmosphere rather than people, and it's visually distinct from the FHSA walking shot.

## 5. FHSA vs. RRSP — "The ultimate comparison"
**File:** insight-savings.png

> Editorial photograph, a kitchen table in morning light with a coffee cup, an open notebook, and a pen. Soft light streaming through a window, casting gentle shadows. A small plant nearby. Warm, clean, minimal. Cream and copper tones. No text visible in notebook, no screens. Photorealistic, overhead angle, shallow depth of field.

**Why:** Savings planning happens at kitchen tables, not on Wall Street trading floors. This feels approachable and personal — someone sitting down to think about their future. Replaces the dark moody office flatlay with something that matches the site's warm palette.

---

## General Notes
- All images should be generated at **1:1 (square)** aspect ratio
- Export at a minimum of **800x800px** — can always scale down for the site
- If Grok gives you anything with text baked into the image, regenerate — the site uses CSS overlays for text
- Aim for images that could pass as real editorial photography, not illustrations or heavy AI styling
- If a prompt isn't landing right, try adding "shot by a lifestyle photographer for an architecture magazine" to nudge the tone
