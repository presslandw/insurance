# Insight Thumbnail Prompts — Round 2 (Alternate Styles)

Same five articles, different creative directions. Mix and match with the Round 1 prompts — pick whichever version lands best per thumbnail.

All images: **1:1 square**, minimum **800x800px**, no text or logos in image.

---

## Style A: Illustrated Editorial

Warm, hand-drawn illustration style with muted watercolour textures. Think New Yorker magazine covers or children's book warmth — sophisticated but approachable. Works well if photorealism keeps looking too stock.

---

### 1. FHSA — "Your secret weapon for a first home down payment"

> Warm watercolour illustration, a young couple planting a small tree in front of a cozy house, golden afternoon light. Soft muted palette of cream, sage green, and copper. Gentle brushstroke textures, slightly whimsical, editorial magazine cover style. No text.

### 2. Term vs. Whole Life — "Term vs. Whole Life explained"

> Warm watercolour illustration, a large sheltering tree with deep roots visible underground, a family having a picnic beneath it. One half of the tree is in full summer leaf, the other half shows autumn colours. Cream, copper, and forest green palette. Gentle and inviting, editorial style. No text.

**Why:** The two halves of the tree visually represent the two types of coverage — one temporary (seasonal), one enduring (deep roots). Communicates the concept without being literal.

### 3. Disability — "Protecting your ability to earn"

> Warm watercolour illustration, a pair of well-worn work boots sitting by a front door, morning light streaming in. A lunch box and thermos nearby. Copper and amber tones, cream background. Tender, dignified, editorial illustration style. No text.

### 4. Mortgage — "Bank vs. Independent: Which coverage is safe?"

> Warm watercolour illustration, two paths diverging from a small house — one path leads to a large imposing bank building, the other to a warm glowing cottage with an open door. Soft earth tones, copper highlights on the cottage path. Storybook feel, editorial style. No text.

### 5. Savings — "FHSA vs. RRSP: The ultimate comparison"

> Warm watercolour illustration, a glass jar on a windowsill slowly filling with golden coins, a small plant growing out of the top. Morning light, soft shadows. Cream, gold, and sage palette. Hopeful and quiet, editorial magazine style. No text.

## Style B: Cinematic Landscape / Atmosphere

No people at all. Moody, cinematic BC landscapes and textures that evoke the emotional territory of each article. These work well as background-style thumbnails where the text overlay does the heavy lifting.

---

### 1. FHSA — "Your secret weapon for a first home down payment"

> Cinematic photograph, a single craftsman-style house on a quiet tree-lined street in the Pacific Northwest, early morning fog lifting. Golden light breaking through. Shot on medium format film, shallow depth of field on the house. Rich earth tones, greens, warm amber highlights. No people, no text.

### 2. Term vs. Whole Life — "Term vs. Whole Life explained"

> Cinematic photograph, a massive old-growth cedar tree in a BC rainforest, sunlight filtering through the canopy in golden shafts. Looking upward from the base. Rich greens, deep shadows, warm golden highlights. Atmospheric and reverent. Shot on 24mm wide angle, film grain. No people, no text.

**Why:** A centuries-old tree is the most honest visual metaphor for long-term protection you can find. And it's unmistakably British Columbia.

### 3. Disability — "Protecting your ability to earn"

> Cinematic photograph, a worn leather tool belt hanging on a workshop wall hook, late afternoon light from a window casting long shadows. Warm wood tones, copper hardware catching the light. Intimate detail shot, film grain, shallow depth of field. No people, no text.

### 4. Mortgage — "Bank vs. Independent: Which coverage is safe?"

> Cinematic photograph, a house key on a simple wooden table next to a window, soft rain visible outside. Warm interior light contrasting with the cool blue rain. Tight composition, shallow depth of field on the key. Copper and cream tones inside, steel blue outside. No people, no text.

### 5. Savings — "FHSA vs. RRSP: The ultimate comparison"

> Cinematic photograph, a mountain trail in the BC interior at sunrise, the path winding upward through golden grassland with snow-capped peaks in the distance. Warm golden light, long shadows. Epic but personal scale — feels like the beginning of a journey. Shot on 35mm, film grain. No people, no text.

## Style C: Textured Abstract / Minimal

For a more design-forward look. Textures, shapes, and colour fields rather than literal scenes. Would pair well with bold text overlays.

---

### 1. FHSA — "Your secret weapon for a first home down payment"

> Abstract photograph, close-up of handmade ceramic tiles in warm terracotta and cream arranged in a geometric house-like pattern. Soft natural light, subtle shadows between tiles. Tactile, artisanal texture. Copper and earth tones. No text.

### 2. Term vs. Whole Life — "Term vs. Whole Life explained"

> Abstract photograph, two pieces of fabric — one lightweight linen, one heavyweight wool — overlapping on a wooden surface. Warm natural light, visible weave textures. Cream, oatmeal, and deep navy tones. Minimal, tactile, design-forward. No text.

**Why:** The weight and permanence difference between the two fabrics mirrors the term vs. whole life distinction without saying a word.

### 3. Disability — "Protecting your ability to earn"

> Abstract photograph, a single strong rope knot in natural jute, close-up macro shot against a warm cream background. Dramatic side lighting creating deep texture and shadow. Copper and sand tones. Minimal, powerful. No text.

**Why:** Strength, security, something that holds. Simple and dignified.

### 4. Mortgage — "Bank vs. Independent: Which coverage is safe?"

> Abstract photograph, an old brass lock and a modern steel lock sitting side by side on weathered wood. Warm directional light, shallow depth of field. Copper patina on the brass contrasting with cool steel. Textured, minimal. No text.

### 5. Savings — "FHSA vs. RRSP: The ultimate comparison"

> Abstract photograph, concentric growth rings on a cross-section of a tree trunk, warm honey tones, soft even lighting. Macro detail showing the fine grain texture. Gold and amber palette. Organic, minimal, meditative. No text.

## Prompt Tips for Grok Imagine
- If results look too "AI glossy," add **"shot on film, slight grain, imperfect lighting"**
- If you get text in the image despite saying "no text," try adding **"purely visual, no words, no signage, no labels"**
- For the illustrated style, adding **"printed on cream textured paper"** helps sell the handmade feel
- You can combine styles — e.g., use cinematic landscapes for most thumbnails but the illustrated tree for the life insurance one as a feature card
- Generate 3-4 variations per prompt and pick the best — Grok's first output isn't always its best
