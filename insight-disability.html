Your most valuable asset is your ability to work

By Chris Walker • 4 min read

When people think of their "assets," they usually think of their home, their car, or their RRSP. But for most workers in British Columbia, their actual greatest asset is their future earning potential.

If you are 35 years old and earning $75,000 a year, you have over $2.2 million in future earnings ahead of you. Disability insurance is simply the way you protect that multi-million dollar asset from the unexpected.

The Gap in Traditional Coverage

Many professionals assume they are "covered" through a group plan at work. While group plans are a great start, they often have significant limitations: they may only cover 50% of your income, they might have a cap on monthly payments, and most importantly, they end the moment you leave your job.

For the self-employed, entrepreneurs, and contractors in the Fraser Valley, the risk is even higher. If you stop working, the revenue stops, but the mortgage and household bills do not.

Why Lifestyle Protection Matters

Individual Disability Insurance provides a tax-free monthly benefit that replaces a portion of your income if you encounter a health challenge—whether it's a physical injury or a serious illness. It ensures that while you focus on recovery, your lifestyle, your home, and your family's daily needs remain protected.

We focus on "Own Occupation" coverage—meaning if you can't perform the specific duties of your job, the policy pays out, even if you could technically do something else.
